Experiments with a Toy Loom

May 11 2022

While in Black Mountains my roommate Daniela and I went to the thrift store. We didn't find much but just as we were about to leave we spotted a toy loom.

It instantly caught my attention. I’ve always loved trying new things and during the week I made a small tapestry using a carboard loom I made from the side of a box in the art room. The packaging of the toy loom looked old, worn, yellowed, but the wooden loom inside looked like it was well maintained. My good fortune, I guess.

I didn’t have a clue how to use it but still, at only 5 bucks who could say no?

We explored downtown Black Mountain for a little bit. The mountains are so scenic it’s easy to see how artists could find inspiration there. We popped into a couple of shops before heading to the yarn store. They had so many brilliantly hued gorgeous yarns.

We were mistaken for people participating in a “yarn crawl”, which just so happened to be going on throughout western North Carolina the same weekend we were in town. I was amazed that something like that even existed.

Daniela jokingly said finding the toy loom and the yarn crawl were all good signs of a burgeoning fiber arts career. Serendipity stacked on serendipity.

I picked out two beautiful hanks of yarn and we went back to the lodge

Fastforward to being back home from Black Mountain for a couple of days. I haven’t made any art. The 9-5 has taken over and I feel stuck once again. Except, I’m not. Because now I have a really cool community of people I know I can lean on. I take out the toy loom and it’s like looking at a particle collider. How does this work?

I watch some videos on threading a loom. There is so much terminology to learn: heddle, weft, shed, warp. The pieces don’t seem to fit in my head.

It takes me two days but eventually I get the courage to start threading the loom. Once I start it is engrossing, and it’s fun to see how far you can get. I made a small little sample to see whether I could actually figure out how the loom worked. Once I’m done I put it on my wall with other treasures I’ve collected over the past couple of weeks.

I don’t know about being a prolific fiber artist, but I like learning new things. I want to do more weaving but I’m also interested in learning other types of arts and crafts too. I’d like to learn about ceramics and screen printing. I’ve never really been good at focusing on one thing, but I hope this won’t be the last of my little fiber arts experiments...
