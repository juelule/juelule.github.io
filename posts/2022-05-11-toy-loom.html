Experiments with a Toy Loom

May 11 2022

While in Black Mountains my roommate Daniela and I went to the thrift store. We didn't find much but just as we were about to leave we spotted a toy loom.

There was a student in Black Mountain, Emily, who inspired me to use the yarn in the art room to crochet and to weave something with a cardboard loom. But I never dreamed that I would continue to weave after the week was over.

Still, it was a huge coincidence that we had found a toy loom in such a tiny little store at the same time I started weaving. I didn’t know how to use a loom but it was only 5 bucks, I could probably figure it out, so why not?

Afterwards Daniela and I walked to downtown Black Mountain and visited a yarn shop. There were some beautiful yarns, with all kinds of different textures and brilliant hues. The ladies who ran the shop thought I was a part of the yarn crawl that just so happened to be going on in Western North Carolina at the same time that we were in town.

Serendipity stacked on serendipity!

I explained that I was in in town for an art program and that I had just bought a toy loom at the thrift store and I wanted to purchase some wool for it. They sounded just as excited and fascinated that I had picked They also thought it was pretty cool and helped me pick out two beautiful hanks of yarn.

Fastforward to today. I’ve been back for a couple of days and I haven’t really made much art. The 9-5 has taken over and I feel as stuck as I did before. Except, I’m not because now I have a really cool community of people I know I can lean on. I take out the toy loom and it’s like looking at a particle collider. How does this work?

I watch some videos on threading a loom. There is so much terminology to learn: heddle, weft, shed, warp. The pieces don’t seem to fit in my head.

I don’t know what to do but I realize that I just need to start. So I start threading the loom. I probably didn't thread it correctly but a big part of what keeps me from starting is the fear of messing up so I say fuck it! Just see how far you can get.

I got pretty far! I was actually able to weave something very simple. It looks kind of shitty but at least it’s done. I made some mistakes but I don't think it's bad for my first attempt. I put it up on my wall with other treasures I’ve collected over the past couple of weeks.

I want to weave more things, but I'm not sure what to make next. I want to do different kinds of crafts. I'd love to learn ceramics, printmaking, sculpture. I like the sensory approach, it feels so good to quiet your brain down and just let your hands work. I promise this won't be the end. My dreams are full of fabric, paint, clay, and embroidery floss...
