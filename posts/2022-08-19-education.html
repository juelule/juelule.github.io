Experiments in Education II

August 19 2022

This summer I decided to sign up for some classes to learn new skills to varying degrees of success. The first class I signed up was a Film class at the Light Factory here in Charlotte. It was the only in person class I took. The instructor Mert was pretty cool. We learned how to develop our own film which I thought was pretty cool. It’s one thing to be able to take your own photos but to also be able to develop them is another.

I took a Cartooning class because I want to make a comic in the future but I feel like I don’t really have the confidence in my drawing to do that. This one was online and through the 92nd Street Y.

I really liked this class and our instructor Josh. I made some comics but they were mostly personal comics, I wish I would’ve made some fiction comics but I’m having a hard time coming up with ideas. I wish I had made more comics, because I haven’t done any since then, but maybe in the future I’ll take the class again.

Josh would show us some comics he thought were cool and showed us different techniques to use. I made like three comics through the class which is more than I’ve made in my entire life.

My least favorite class was my risography class. I also took this one online, but this time through SVA. I bought a zine that was riso printed(rhymes with miso, as in miso soup) and thought the colors were so cool, I knew I wanted to learn how to make riso prints.

I never went to art school so I'm trying to basically make my own art school. It's hard to do when you are also working a 9-5 but it's also a lot less pressure and probably less expensive.

There are a lot of advantages to going to art school, I actually can’t think of any advantage of the approach I’m trying, but I also don’t have the time or motivation to apply and enroll in an art school, especially since I have a job and am not independently wealthy. I could probably find the same resources online for free but I feel like it’s more time consuming and I like having a teacher there to help keep me engaged.

I don’t know why I can’t just commit to one thing. But I guess that’s the point of experimenting. Trying new things, despite not knowing what the outcome will be, just to see what comes out of it.
