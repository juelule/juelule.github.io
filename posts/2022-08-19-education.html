Experiments in Education II

August 19 2022

I’m trying to understand myself. I don’t think I get why I am the way I am and why I do the things I do. Why can’t I focus on one thing? I don’t think I’ve ever really been able to. I don't feel like I belong anywhere, but I think being an artist is about creating a place to belong.

Black Mountain was really important because it showed me a way forward, a way for me to believe I can do this, that I can become an artist without going to a formal art school despite approaching it in an admittedly conventional way.

I took three classes this summer: a photography class, a comic class and a printmaking class.

My photography class was called Black and White Darkroom. As you may have noticed I love taking film photos. I’m not very good at it but that really doesn’t stop me.

One day I will learn how to use a camera. I mean, I have to: I have so fucking many of them. I have three film cameras right now, an SLR and two point and shoots. I usually just drop off the film at the local shop and get the scans emailed to me though, I had never developed my own.

Developing film is more formally about experimentation. Testing out different amounts of chemicals and seeing what happens, introducing different effects to the final image.It’s also magical. Taking contact sheets out of the enlarger, watching images start to appear after being dipped in developer, it's really cool. I love being in the darkroom and being so hands on with the creation of photos. This was definitely a very precious experience.

The comic class was called Cartooning for Beginners. I’ve never really drawn a comic before but I’d like to, some day, write a whole anthology of different stories.

Josh was pretty cool he would expose us to different comics and techniques and we would have to use those ideas in our own comics. I also liked how we were encouraged to draw during class and given critiques on how we could improve or do things differently. As a beginner I really like being challenged and introduced to new things.

I wish I had kept up making comics since then but unfortunately I’ve been preoccupied with other things. Hopefully I eventually can get back into it.

Lastly, my printmaking class which was also online, an incredibly difficult thing to do I learned, was called Introduction to Risography. It involved learning how to setup files online for a risograph machine. It was my least favorite class because it was not hands on.

I wish I could've actually worked on a real life riso machine but there really aren't any public riso machines here in Charlotte. Hopefully that changes in the future.

I have been in love with risograph printing for a while now, I love the colors and the texture, it looks so beautiful and if it wasn’t so expensive I would’ve bought a risograph machine but for now I rely on the riso machines of others.

I guess I’m just not in the habit of limiting myself to one thing. I live in a world and a future full of possibilities.

Continuing to make my own way is not even a question, but a certainty. I don’t have any choice but to try and gain the tools and accrue the knowledge to make my dreams and work come true.

I’m in the infancy of my work after all. I’m only 23. An early career artist. I don’t owe anything to anyone except, maybe, myself. And that debt will only last a lifetime.
