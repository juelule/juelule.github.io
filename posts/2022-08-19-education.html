Experiments in Education II

August 19 2022

To continue the self-education motif, I signed up for a couple of classes over the summer.

I never went to art school and I feel like it puts me at such a disadvantage despite people telling me art school is not as important as I think. So I’m trying to do like my own version of art school by taking classes I think I would’ve taken if I had gone.

OThe first class I signed up for was a Darkroom class at the Light Factory here in Charlotte. I've been shooting film for almost a year now, but I usually just drop it off at the lab and someone else develops it. I wanted to learn how the process actually works, because it seemed so mystifying.

Our instructor, Mert, taught us how film was developed, how contact sheets were made and how enlargers worked. It was really cool to actually work in a darkroom. When we put our prints in the developer and images started appearing it was like magic!

I really liked this class, and I hope I get the chance to work in the darkroom more in the future.

The second class I took was a Cartooning class through the 92nd Street Y. I wanted to start making comics, but I didn’t feel like I could draw well enough to do that. I signed up for this class hoping I could get some .

The comics I ended up making for class were mostly personal, but I really want to make more like science fiction/fantasy. I think the thing I struggle with the most is coming up with scripts and blocking people in a panel. Josh would give us a prompt for a comic to make and we could interpret it anyway we wanted, but for some reason I could only figure out ways to relate it to my self.

Josh would show us some comics he thought were interesting and the techniques the artists were using that we should also use. I made like three comics in the class which is more than I’ve made in my entire life.

I’m a little embarrassed about them so I’ll only share a little bit. I hope I can keep making comics because I definitely need to improve my drawing skills.

My least favorite class was my risography class I took through SVA. I had seen a riso (rhymes with miso) printed zine, fell in love with the colors and decided I wanted to learn more.

Riso is basically a type of printer sold by the Riso Kagaku corporation. Originally it was just for schools and churches to make cheap copies. Somewhere along the way artists noticed the beautiful and vibrant colored inks and started using it to make art.

The reason I didn't enjoy my class, I think, is because we didn’t actually make anything in the class. We just learned how to prepare files for six weeks in the eventuality that we, or the local print shop, ever get access to a riso printer. Unfortunately there isn’t really a riso press here in charlotte so I had to find one online.

I was able to find one that was taking clients, did a quick shitty design (which I really hate) and managed to actually make some riso prints. Now I have 25 of them that I don’t know what to do with, I don’t think anyone would buy them if I actually wanted to sell them.

I’m not sure why why I can’t just commit to one thing. But I guess that’s the point of experimenting. Trying new things, despite not knowing what the outcome will be, just to see what comes out of it.

I hope I can actually put these skills to use instead of acquiring them and then moving on. It’s sort of difficult because none of these classes really build on top of each other even though there’s some overlap. I need to do some sort of project where I try to merge all these skills together. I’m gonna take some more classes next semester, I already signed up for a couple of them and I’m hopefully they help me grow as an artist.
