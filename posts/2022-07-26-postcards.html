Experiments in Futility

July 26 2022

In Black Mountain, we had a community bulletin board where people could share flyers for their impromptu classes, upcoming events, or ask for rides to town. As our week was coming to an end people wrote down their addresses and contact info to keep in touch. I decided I wanted to send mail to everyone who wrote down their address, and I thought postcards would be the perfect thing.

I've always loved postcards. Every time I go somewhere new I try to look for a postcard as a souvenir. They’re simple keepsakes and you can find them most everywhere. Making them yourself, however, is a little less simple.

I didn’t document much of the process of making the postcards, which I really regret, but it took me nearly two months. I printed out the photos I took during my week in the mountains, then I bought card stock at the craft store, cut them into postcard shapes and glued them to the back of the photos. I did a linocut print for the divided back design. I had never done a linocut before, but I was inspired to try it out because of something someone made in the art room.

I think the most difficult part for me was writing messages to people. I had forgotten how profound the entire experience was. Especially the last couple of days when everyone was leaving. I remember almost not being able to say goodbye. I would tear up as I thought of never seeing people who I really grew to care for again. I've always had the problem of loving too quickly, the feelings I have cut me in half sometimes.

Remembering and reliving those memories made me so grateful that they happened. At the same time it felt like I couldn’t fit everything into words, and I definitely couldn’t fit them onto a 4 x 6 postcard. I wish there was a way that you could transfer how you feel to someone and they could just understand. When people read what you write can it ever truly and utterly convey the love in your heart?

I think what I experienced in Black Mountain really was the catalyst for what I’ve been doing lately. It breathed new life into me. And I’m really proud of myself for finishing this project and extending the Black Mountain magic. It’s the first major project I’ve worked on since being back home. My intention with this work is that people will know that I’m thinking of them and missing them dearly…
