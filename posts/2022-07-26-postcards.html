Experiments in Futility

July 26 2022

Did you know people who study and collect postcards are called deltiologists? I’ve always loved postcards. Whenever I have the privilege of traveling, I always make sure to get a postcard. They’re pretty cheap and ubiquitous, so they seem like the perfect souevenir.

I decided to make postcards for the friends I made in Black Mountain and it took me nearly two months to finish.

Unfortunately I didn't document most of the process of making them but I made them by printing photos I had taken during the week and glued some cardstock to the back. Then I made a linocut for the divided back design.

Even if it took two months it felt like it went pretty quickly. I need to learn to take my time, as long as I'm making progress, but I don't think like that. If it were up to me I would've tried to finish this in a week and because I felt like I couldn't finish this in such a short turn around I wasn't sure if I was really frustrated with myself. sometimes it feels like

I took a good amount of photos, but unfortunately didn’t capture every single person in our little community, but hopefully next time I will be make sure to take a photo of everyone.

I think the hardest part was writing messages, it was like I had forgotten how profound some of the experiences were until I rememberd saying goodbye to some of my friends, almost not able to keep it together. I remember hugging people and my voice trembling as I said goodbye. The love I had for people felt like it could cut me in half.

I sent a lot of people cards but I didn't get a lot of responses which is okay because I made the effort and there is still time for people to write me back. I have a tendency to believe that people dont want me around and that i'm just a tough person to love but I wanted to prove myself wrong

I'm just hoping that people will see that I am still thinking of them and that it brightens their day a little. Even if my words are underwhelming.

Originally i was going to call this an exercise in gratitude but later i thought maybe that wouldn’t be appropriate. I don’t think i was adequately able to articulate how grateful i was for the experiences i had in black mountain this past may. Especially not in a 4x6 postcard. Words could not capture the magnitudes of love i felt for everyone involved. Not even in years would i be able to do so. And yet i could not help but continue on with this exercise in failure.

There's no way for me to express how deeply these experiences have shaped me, and I don't think i came even remotely close to communicating that to each individual person, but I tried my best and i hope people know how deeply I love them...
