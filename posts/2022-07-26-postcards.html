Experiments in Futility

July 26 2022

During my week in Black Mountain, we had a bulletin board where people shared their addresses to keep in touch after the week was over. I thought it’d be really cool to make something to send to everyone who wrote down their address.

I ended up making postcards because I really love postcards. Every time I go anywhere I always look for a postcard to get as a souvenir.

I didn’t document much of the process of making the postcards, which I really regret, but it took me nearly two months to make them. I took photos during the week, and when I got home I printed them out at the local film lab. Then I bought card stock at the craft store, cut them into postcard shapes and glued them to the back of the photos. The divided back design with the little flower and stamp square was made using a linocut stamp. I had never done linocut before, but I was inspired to try it out because of a print someone made in the art room.

I think the most difficult part for me was writing messages to people. I had forgotten how profound the entire experience was. Especially the last couple of days when everyone was saying goodbye. I remember almost not being able to keep it together as we said our farewells. My voice trembled as I wished people a safe trip back home. The love I had for people felt like it could cut me in half.

Remembering and reliving those memories made me so grateful that they happened. At the same time it felt like I couldn’t fit my feelings into words, and I definitely couldn’t fit them onto a 4 x 6 postcard. I wish there was a way to print out your feelings and send them to someone and they could just understand. When people read what you write can it ever truly and utterly convey the love in your heart?

I think what I experienced in Black Mountain really was the catalyst for what I’ve been doing lately. It breathed new life into me. And I’m really proud of myself for finishing this project and trying to keep the spirit of Black Mountain alive, it’s like the first major project I’ve worked on since being back home. My wish with this work is that people will know that I’m thinking of them and missing them dearly…
