Experiments in Doing Nothing

May 18 2022

Recently, I went camping for the first time with a bunch of strangers. We hung out at Marrow Mountain for two days. I had never been camping before but it was such a refreshing experience. We went on a small hike to see the quarry and a longer hike to see the dam at Lake Tillery.

Being out in nature feels amazing. Looking at trees and foliage, fresh air circulating through the lungs, moving in a way that feels good. I wish I could do more of that.

I feel like my mind is always going a mile a minute. It’s hard to quiet it down but when I’m out in nature, or just in a different environment entirely it feels a lot easier to control the noise.

I cannot escape the productivity scam. Doing nothing feels wrong, I feel so guilty thinking of all the things I'm not doing. Yet it’s so necessary towards recharging and finding inspiration.

It’s impossible for me to do nothing and I just need to accept that. I need to take more field trips like this, to reset, to enjoy the world that is a lot more than the little strip of life we experience day to day. It’s a necessary disruption, an expansion of consciousness.

There are so many magical people and places in this world and I wish I could meet each and every one of them, but I feel like the mundanity of living keeps pulling me down to it’s level and I’m unable to recognize the beauty of what is already around me unless I leave my usual stomping grounds.

I need to carve out more time for field trips, it feels like a really important part of the artistic process to get out of your normal routine. I feel like if I don’t make time for it I’ll never make any art at all. And sometimes I feel like making art is really the only form of redemption I have.
