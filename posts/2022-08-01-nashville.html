Field Trip: Nashville

August 01 2022

I went to Nashville for my half birthday to get away from myself— and to take a bunch of photos.

Travel is one of the few things I look forward to anymore.

Don’t get me wrong, I like making art, it’s probably the only thing that makes life worth living. But lately I’ve realized that probably the reason I can’t focus on one medium is because I’m not good at any of them and I’m trying to find a medium I am good at.

Do I even enjoy making art or do I just see it as a way of redeeming myself? A conversation for another day.

I started my trip to Nashville on Broadway, which is a street littered with bars, restaurants, cowboy boot shops and honky tonks. The delightfully named honky tonks are bars that play live music at all hours of the day and are free to get into. Every band I heard sounded amazing, live music is a treat back home usually if you go to a restaurant or bar they’re playing pre-recorded music. but on broadway you’ll hear live performers everywhere you go, their voices sometimes blending mellifulously together— and sometimes they just sound like noise! It’s incredibly lively and fun.

Most of it is country music, but sometimes you’ll hear rock. I even heard a band at Rippy’s covering Rihanna and Lauryn Hill. I wouldn’t say I’m a country fan per se, but I love music. I don’t care what genre as long as it sounds good to me.

I went to the Country Music Hall of Fame Museum because Nashville is music city and I really like museums.

I think it’s interesting to think about how music has developed. Like one day our ancestors were just making noises, with their hands with their mouths, and then they realized that certain noises sounded good together. They realized that making noises in certain patterns can also sound good. And they invented instruments to make more complex noises and patterns.

It makes me think how all this started so simply. Humans love for novelty leads to people making sounds with their hands, with their mouths, with whatever they can find. People form bonds playing music, it probably lead to greater cohesion.

I think music posseses such a divine quality, beyond that which words can convey, it can be incredibly powerful.

I spent a couple hours walking around the Cumberland River that evening, trying to find the right shot of the Shelby Ave Bridge

During the waning daylight hours the neon signs on Broadway draw bachelorettes, wannabe cowboys, and drunk tourists like moths to a flame all ready to party the night away.

I stayed at a hotel called The Graduate. It’s a chain hotel who’s shtick is that they’re decorated differently based on the city and universities their near, with the tagline ‘We’re all Students’ You can probably tell why I chose it. But beyond that the Nashville location is pretty much a love letter to Dolly Parton who I admire a lot.

The hotel has a really cool rooftop bar called White Limozeen, named after one of Dolly’s albums, I had lunch there for my half birthday and met with sort of the same people as on Broadway, bachelorette’s, teenagers, and instagram driven influencers. Does any other type of people come to Nashville?

I did enjoy the hotel though, all the details from the cowboy boots on the lobby carpet, to the dolly portrait above the canopy bed, the lights and decor in the lobby I thought it was such a darling place to stay.

I think Starland Vintage was the only clothing store I went to during the trip but it was really the only one needed. They also had the same Dolly Parton veneration level as The Graduate and I loved looking at all the clothing and furniture they smartly curated.

I decided that I really wanted to buy a pair of cowboy boots at one of the countless boot shops on or around Broadway. I ended up finding a nice pair in the basement at French’s.

Not far from Vanderbuilt is Centennial Park, home to a replica of the Parthenon in Athens. I figure this would be the closest I’d probably get to either Parthenon so I should try and visit.

I also went to the National Museum of African American Music. Roots music, hip hop, rock, pop, gospel, basically every form of popular music derives from African music. I also thought for parities sake if I went to the Country Music museum I should go to the NMAAM

It really is amazing how many genres they put into this exhibit, but also how quickly one genre evolved into a new one, from gospel, to blues, to jazz, to r&b, to hip hop it’s really incredible.

And that was basically my trip to Nashville. I feel like I learned a lot about music, and about myself.

I didn’t get to go to East Nashville even though I had plans to go to a bunch of different places there, I ended up staying pretty close to Broadway. But there’s always next time.

I had so much fun in Nashville, there was so much to do it almost felt like four days was not enough.

I think the alternate reality I was accessing was one where I spend my days listening to music, making music, and just generally having fun.

I feel like i'm always so tired and sleep deprived when I’m back home. I don't know if I even enjoy what I'm doing or if I'm just doing it because i think it's what i'm supposed to be doing. But having some distance from those thoughts and responsibilities feels nice. You have the choice to think clearly or not think at all.

The world feels like it's full of magic and music again. Not sure how long till it wears off but for now I'm pretty happy.
