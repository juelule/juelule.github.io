Field Trip: Nashville

August 01 2022

Traveling is one of the few things I look forward to anymore.

Don’t get me wrong, I like making art! It’s probably the only thing that makes life worth living, but lately I’ve realized that the reason I can’t focus on one medium is probably because I’m not good at any of them.

Do I even enjoy making art? Or do I just see it as a way of redeeming myself? A conversation for another day.

I feel like I'm trying to find any excuse to just flee my life and my responsibilities. I want to find a place where I can be done, a life where I don't have to find excuses, where I'm happy, but that's going to take quite a while to happen I suppose.

What's that thing Mitski said? I should move to a brand new city and teach myself how to die.

Nashville, the land of cowboys, songwriters, hot chicken and goo goo clusters. And I really enjoyed my time there.

I had so much fun in Nashville, I went to a couple museums, listened to a ton of live music, brought some cowboy boots and got shitfaced.

I like Nashville because it feels like there's so much life there. There were so many people and there was so much music.

I was really inspired by all the live music, I loved the honky-tonks and bars that had live bands at all hours of the day.

It made me wish that I could play music. I’ve never played an instrument before but I’ve always loved music which is the main reason that I wanted to go to Nashville.

I feel as most people do that I could be doing so much more. That all this busy work, and systems of tiered access, disparity between classes, and endless indentured servitude just to have a decent life and get ahead don’t matter, they should be abolished and we should be able to take care of each other. I could be doing so much more with my life, living so much more, experiencing things beyond what I’m capable of experiencing right now. I could be having fun, I could be making music, I could be making art— I could be doing something worthwhile! I know most people feel like this but doesn’t it make you just so frustrated?

It feels like you’re only living in small pockets in-between periods of being half alive.

Maybe I should just move to a brand new city and teach myself how to die.

I wish I was in a place where I don't have to do so many things I don't want to do. Is it a surprise that I feel like my brain is constantly working against me? I ignore so many signals I'm being sent day in and day out about what is making me unhappy i feel like im in a constant state of self betrayal

I think the alternate reality I was accessing was one where I spend my days listening to music, making music, and just generally having fun.

The world feels like it's full of magic and music again. Not sure how long till it wears off but for now I'm pretty happy.
