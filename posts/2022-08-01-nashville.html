Field Trip: Nashville

August 01 2022

Traveling is one of the few things I look forward to anymore.

Don’t get me wrong, I still like making art-- it’s probably one of the few things that makes life worth living, but lately I’ve realized that the reason I can’t focus on one medium is probably because I’m not good at any of them.

Do I even enjoy making art? Or do I just see it as a way of redeeming myself? Let’s leave that thought behind for now…

I feel like I'm always trying to find any excuse to flee my life and my responsibilities. And that’s probably why lately, what’s been taking up the most mental real estate is planning trips to new places. Is that healthy? Probably not. But like I said before, I don’t have the answers to everything. I’m still figuring it out.

I’ve been thinking a lot about the lyric I should move to a brand new city and teach myself how to die lately. I wonder if Nashville is the city Mitski chose in the end.

Nashville: the land of cowboys, songwriters, hot chicken and goo goo clusters. I decided to go to Nashville because I wanted to go to a city that had some connection to music. At first I was gonna try to go to both New Orleans and Nashville, but I ended up only having time for one city and decided to go to New Orleans another time.

I had a lot of fun in Nashville. I went to a couple museums, listened to a ton of live music, brought some cowboy boots and got shitfaced for my half-birthday. I could see why people would want to go and live there, it just seems like a really good time.

The live music especially was really inspiring. Everywhere you went on Broadway, the main party street in Nashville lined with honky-tonks and bars, there were people performing at all hours of the day. I loved the energy of the musicians, and for some reason live music just sounds so much better— I mean that’s why people pay money to go to concerts right? But it was all free, unless you chose to tip them.

It made me wish that I could play music. I don’t know how to play an instrument, and even if I could I don’t think I would ever be brave enough to play in public. But I just felt like what I admired about the people I saw was that they looked like they were having a lot of fun playing, and even if I just made music by myself it would be just as enjoyable.

I stayed at this themed hotel called the Graduate. Their motto is “We Are All Students” because they like to set up shop in college towns (Vanderbilt’s right down the road). I really liked that idea for obvious reasons.

I never went to college, and don’t have any current intentions of going, but I love learning. I thought that’s what would happen in Nashville despite not doing anything particularly academic.

I don’t believe that learning only happens in formal situations. Playtime can be learning time. I think the times when you’re enjoying yourself are the times when you can gain insight and realize something profound. And I hadn’t enjoyed myself as much as I had in Nashville in a long time

The Graduate is a pink, country themed hotel with a Dolly Parton themed restaurant on the top floor. I felt right at home.

I remember singing a lot of Dolly songs while I was there, I especially really liked Why’d You Come in Here Looking Like That, I think that was the Dolly song I listened to the most during my trip. I also found out about other female country singers like Allison Ponthier and Kacey Musgraves.

The idea of having to go home really started gnawing at my soul. I started imaging what it would be like if I didn’t go back. The world I live in is one where I’m constantly ignoring the signals coming from inside and around me.

I feel, as most people probably do, that I could be doing so much more. All this busy work, systems of tiered access, growing disparity between the poor and rich, and endless indentured servitude just to have a decent life and get ahead don’t matter, just feels like a huge waste of time you can never escape from.

I could be doing so much more with my life, living so much more, experiencing things beyond what I’m experiencing right now. I could be having fun, I could be making music, I could be making art— I could be doing something worthwhile! I know most people feel like this but doesn’t it make you just so frustrated?

It feels like you’re only living in small pockets in-between periods of being mostly dead.

Maybe I should move to a brand new city and teach myself how to die.

I wish I was in a place where I don't have to do so many things I don't want to do. Is it a surprise that I feel like my brain is constantly working against me? I ignore so many signals I'm being sent day in and day out about what is making me unhappy I feel like I’m in a constant state of self betrayal. I need to learn how to honor my feelings and listen to what I really want...

I tried finding some place that might sell zines in Nashville but couldn't. The closest thing I could find was the Hatch Show Print ship which was also in the Country Music Hall of Fame.

It was a really cool tour, they told us the history of the shop and how the letterpresses worked. We even got to use a mini letterpress to make part of a poster keepsake.

I had done linocut before at this point, but I didn’t know about letterpress. I have a dream of opening a print shop in Charlotte one day, primarily doing riso and screen prints but maybe it’ll also include letterpress.

Maybe the alternate reality I was accessing on this trip was one where I spend my days listening to music, making music, and just generally having fun.

It'll take time to figure out what all these pieces mean. How do they fit together? And how can I build the life I want? How does anyone do it?

At least for a little bit, the world feels like it's full of magic and music. Not sure how long till it wears off but for now, I’m full of joy.
