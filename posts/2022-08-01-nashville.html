Field Trip: Nashville

August 01 2022

Travelling is one of the few things I look forward to anymore.

Don’t get me wrong. I like making art, it’s probably the only thing that makes life worth living. But lately I’ve realized that the reason I can’t focus on one medium is probably because I’m not good at any of them.

Do I even enjoy making art? Or do I just see it as a way of redeeming myself? A conversation for another day.

I feel like I'm trying to find any excuse to just flee my life and my responsibilities. I want to find a place where I can be done, a life where I don't have to find excuses, where I'm happy, but that's going to take quite a while to happen I suppose.

Suffice to say, I went to Nashville for my half birthday to get away from myself— and to take a bunch of photos.

I spent a lot of tip listening to and learning about music and just having fun for once. My first stop was the Country Music Hall of Fame to learn about music.

I think it’s interesting to think about how music developed. Like one day our ancestors were just making noises, with their hands or their mouths, and then they realized that certain noises sounded good together. They realized that making noises in certain patterns creates a rhythm. And they invented instruments to make more complex noises and patterns.

People really liked music it made people feel safe or happy, or allowed them to express fear and sorrow. Human love of novelty means that there were innumerable ways for people to just create different permutations of those expressions. Some people say music probably came before speech.

Now there's a whole industry dedicated to making money off something that every human

It makes me think how all this started so simply. Humans love for novelty leads to people making sounds with their hands, with their mouths, with whatever they can find. People form bonds playing music, it probably lead to greater cohesion.

Anyways I think I was more interested in my thoughts than I was in the actual museum. The musuem was filled to the brim with memorbilia, dresses, nudie suits, records, guitars. There was an exhibit on the Southern California rock-country scene that included the Byrds and the Flying Burrito Brothers but it wasn't open yet. I wish it had been because that sounds like something I would've been interested in.

Nearby is Broadway, which is a street littered with bars, restaurants, cowboy boot shops and honky tonks. The delightfully named honky tonks are bars that play live music at all hours of the day and are free to get into. Every band I heard sounded amazing, live music is a treat back home usually if you go to a restaurant or bar they’re playing pre-recorded music. but on broadway you’ll hear live performers everywhere you go, their voices sometimes blending mellifulously together— and sometimes blending into grating noise.

Most of it is country music, but sometimes you’ll hear rock. I even heard a band at Rippy’s covering Rihanna and Lauryn Hill. I wouldn’t say I’m a country fan per se, but I love music. I don’t care what genre as long as it sounds good to me.

During the waning daylight hours the neon signs on Broadway draw bachelorettes, wannabe cowboys, and country freaks like moths to a flame all ready to party the night away.

I stayed at a Dolly Parton themed hotel called The Graduate. It's a chain who's tagline is 'We're all Students' because they tend to go to places that are nearby universities, in this case Vanderbuilt. I loved that idea 'We're all Students' it fits with the theme of a field trip perfectly.

The hotel has a really cool rooftop bar called White Limozeen, named after one of Dolly’s albums, I had lunch there for my half birthday and met with sort of the same people as on Broadway, bachelorette’s, teenagers, and instagram influencers. Does any other type of people come to Nashville?

I did enjoy the hotel though, all the details from the cowboy boots on the lobby carpet, to the Dolly portrait above the canopy bed, the lights and decor in the lobby. I thought it was such a darling place to stay.

Starland Vintage was the only clothing store I went to during the trip but it also continued the theme of Dolly hero worship, they had Dolly artwork and Dolly merchandise for sale

Not far from Vanderbuilt is Centennial Park, home to a replica of the Parthenon in Athens. I figure this would be the closest I’d probably get to either Parthenon so I should try and visit.

My last day I went to the Hatch Show Print shop. We went on a tiny tour and were also able to make our own prints. I had never heard of letterpress printing before so it was really cool to see what that was. It made me want to do more printmaking and learn about different printmaking methods.

I also went to the National Museum of African American Music. Roots music, hip hop, rock, pop, gospel, basically every form of popular music derives from African music. I also thought for parities sake if I went to the Country Music museum I should go to the NMAAM

It really is amazing how many genres they put into this exhibit, but also how quickly one genre evolved into a new one, from gospel, to blues, to jazz, to r&b, to hip hop it’s really incredible.

I really liked my time in Nashville. I feel like i'm always so tired and sleep deprived when I’m back home. I don't know if I even enjoy what I'm doing or if I'm just doing it because i think it's what I'm supposed to be doing. But having some distance from those thoughts and responsibilities feels nice. You have the choice to think clearly or not think at all.

I think the alternate reality I was accessing was one where I spend my days listening to music, making music, and just generally having fun.

The world feels like it's full of magic and music again. Not sure how long till it wears off but for now I'm pretty happy.
