Field Trip: Nashville

August 01 2022

Traveling is one of the few things I look forward to anymore.

Don’t get me wrong, I still like making art-- it’s probably one of the few things that make life worth living, but lately I’ve realized that the reason I can’t focus on one medium is probably because I’m not good at any of them.

Do I even enjoy making art? Or do I just see it as a way of redeeming myself? Let’s leave that thought behind for now…

I feel like I'm always trying to find any excuse to flee my life and responsibilities. That’s probably why lately, planning trips to new places is taking up most of my mental real estate. Is that healthy? Probably not. But like I said before, I don’t have all the answers. I’m still figuring it out.

I’ve been thinking a lot about the lyric I should move to a brand new city and teach myself how to die. I wonder if Nashville is the city Mitski chose in the end.

Nashville: the land of cowboys, songwriters, hot chicken and goo goo clusters. I decided to go to Nashville because I wanted to go to a city that had some connection to music. At first I was gonna try to go to both New Orleans and Nashville, but I ended up only having time for one city and decided to save NOLA for later.

I had a lot of fun in Nashville. I went to a couple museums, listened to a ton of live music, brought some cowboy boots and got shitfaced for my half-birthday. I could see why people would want to go and live there, it just seems like a good time town.

The live music especially was really inspiring. Everywhere you went on Broadway (the main party street in Nashville lined with honky-tonks and bars) there were live performances at all hours of the day. I loved the energy of the musicians, and for some reason live music just sounds so much better.

It made me wish that I could play music. I don’t know how to play an instrument, and even if I could I don’t think I would ever be brave enough to play in public. But I think what I admired most about the people I saw was that they looked like they were having a lot of fun, and even if I just made music by myself it would be just as enjoyable.

I stayed at this themed hotel called the Graduate. Their motto is “We Are All Students” because they like to set up shop in college towns (Vanderbilt’s right down the road). I really liked that idea for obvious reasons.

I never went to college, and don’t have any current intentions of going, but I love learning. I thought that’s what would happen in Nashville despite not doing anything particularly academic.

I don’t believe that learning only happens in formal situations. Playtime can be learning time. Unstructured time is so important, and what better way to get that than wandering a new city. I think the times when you’re enjoying yourself are the times when you can gain insight and realize something profound. I hadn’t enjoyed myself as much as I had in Nashville in a long time

The Graduate Nashville is a pink, country music themed hotel. From the moment you walk in through the door, all the way to your room and up to the rooftop restaurant named after Dolly Parton's White Limozeen album, it is the most darling little hotels I've ever stayed at. I felt right at home.

I imagined what it would be like if I didn’t go back home. What if I moved to this brand new city and taught myself how to die?

I mean what do I have to go back to? A world where I’m constantly ignoring the signals coming from inside and around me. At what point do I start listening? And at what point is it too late?

Is it a surprise that I feel like my brain is constantly working against me? I ignore so many signals I'm being sent day in and day out about what is making me unhappy. I feel like I’m in a constantly state of self betrayal. I need to learn how to honor my feelings and listen to what I really want... But it's so difficult

I feel, as most people probably do, that I could be doing so much more. All this busy work, systems of tiered access, growing disparity between the poor and rich, and endless indentured servitude just to have a decent life and get ahead don’t matter, just feels like a huge waste of time you can never escape from.

I could be doing so much more with my life, living so much more, experiencing things beyond what I’m experiencing right now. I could be having fun, I could be making music, I could be making art— I could be doing something worthwhile! I know most people feel like this but doesn’t it make you just so frustrated?

It feels like you’re only living in small pockets in-between periods of being mostly dead.

Maybe I  should move to a brand new city and teach myself how to die.

One thing I wished I had been able to do was find a place that sold zines in Nashville. The closest thing I could find was the Hatch Show Print Shop at the Country Music Hall of Fame.

Hatch does a tour where they tell you the history of the shop and the different types of printing they do. They've been around since 1879, once across the street from the famous Ryman Auditorium, the shop would get a lot of work from acts performing at the Ryman and the Grand Ole Opry.

I had done linocut before, but I didn't know too much about letterpress printing until I went to Hatch. One day I hope I get to do more with printmaking, the art they were able to do was so cool.

Maybe the alternate reality I was accessing on this trip was one where I spend my days listening to music, making music, and just generally having fun.

I feel like there's so many ideas here that I'm trying to give birth to. It'll take some time to really figure out what it all means. How does it all fit together? How can I build the life I want? How does anyone?

At least for a little bit, the world feels like it's full of magic and music. Not sure how long till it wears off but for now, I’m full of joy.
