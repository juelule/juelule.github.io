Field Trip: Nashville

August 01 2022

Traveling is one of the few things I look forward to anymore.

Don’t get me wrong-- I still like making art, it’s probably the only thing that makes life worth living, but lately I’ve realized that the reason I can’t focus on one medium is probably because I’m not good at any of them.

Do I even enjoy making art? Or do I just see it as a way of redeeming myself?

I feel like I'm always trying to find any excuse to flee my life and responsibilities. That’s probably why I spend so much time planning trips. Is that healthy? Probably not.

I'm getting to the point where I hate everything I write. I keep writing (and rewriting), about my experiences in Nashville but nothing feels honest.

Writing has to come from a place of honesty. And sometimes that honesty is not possible because you're never honest, not even with yourself.

I think I like what I write when I feel like I've hit upon an insight, like I've figured some sort of truth about myself or the world. But maybe there isn't anything that deep during this trip.

I don't know that I gained any insight in Nashville though. I think I just took a silly little trip.

But I don't want my trips to just be silly little trips. I want them to have meaning, to be places where I can learn something and enrich my life. Because I want to change my life. I want to be transformed.

Living a life full of meaning is so important to me for some reason, and when I feel like that meaning is lacking I'm totally adrift.

Sometimes life feels like an elaborate ruse to make people think you're better off than you really are.

I went to Nashville alone. I did have a good time but I was sort of lonely.

It is worth it though, to have these singular experiences, to grow as a person and learn some things about yourself. I celebrated my half birthday in Nashville and even though I was by myself I still had a really good time.

What I really loved about Nashville was, of course, the music playing everywhere all the time. Listening to so much live music was so fucking cool, it made me realize that I need to learn how to play an instrument.

I also loved Hatch Show Print shop. I tried to look for places that might sell zines but found this place insted. They do letterpress printing and it gave me ideas for a future shop I'd like to open some day in Charlotte.

I feel like I was accessing an alternate reality in Nashville. One where I spend my days listening to music, making music, and just generally having fun. But it didn't transform my life.

Being able to weave your experiences together and seeing the big picture in the tapestry is the apex of one's reflective powers, I think. But it doesn't come easy to me.

I don't yet know what it takes for an epiphany to become life-changing, and I do think realizing that my life can be different is an epiphany, I just don't know what I'm gonna do about it yet.
