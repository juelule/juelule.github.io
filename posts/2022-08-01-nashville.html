Field Trip: Nashville

August 01 2022

Traveling is one of the few things I look forward to anymore.

Don’t get me wrong, I still like making art (it’s probably the only thing that makes life worth living) but lately I’ve realized that the reason I can’t focus on one medium is probably because I’m not good at any of them.

Do I even enjoy making art? Or do I just see it as a way of redeeming myself?

I feel like I'm always trying to find any excuse to flee my life and responsibilities. That’s probably why lately, planning trips to new places is taking up most of my mental real estate. Is that healthy? Probably not. But like I said before, I don’t have all the answers. I’m still figuring it out.

I’ve been thinking a lot about the lyric I should move to a brand new city and teach myself how to die. I wonder if Nashville is the city Mitski chose in the end.

Nashville: the land of cowboys, songwriters, hot chicken and goo goo clusters. I decided to go to Nashville because I wanted to go to a city that had some connection to music. At first I was gonna try to go to both New Orleans and Nashville, but I ended up only having time for one city and decided to save NOLA for later.

I had a lot of fun in Nashville. I went to a couple museums, listened to a ton of live music, brought some cowboy boots and got shitfaced for my half-birthday (23 1/2!). I could see why people would want to move there, I honestly thought about moving myself but haven't made any commitment yet.

The live music especially was really inspiring. I mostly spent my time on Broadway (the main party street in Nashville lined with honky-tonks and bars) where there were live performances at all hours of the day. I loved the energy of the musicians, and for some reason live music just sounds so much better.

It made me wish that I could play music. I don’t know how to play an instrument, and even if I could I don’t think I would ever be brave enough to play in public. But I think what I admired most about the people I saw was that they looked like they were having a lot of fun, even if I just made music by myself it'd probably be fun too.

I stayed at this chain hotel called the Graduate. Their motto is “We Are All Students” because they like to set up shop in college towns (Vanderbilt’s right down the road). I really liked that idea for obvious reasons.

I don’t believe that an education only happens in formal situations. I never went to college, and don’t have any current intentions of going, but I love learning. I do think of these "field trips" as educational experiences despite anything academic happening. I think having a lot of unstructured time, just wandering around a city, really helps me come up with new ideas and reflect on things. It's like a type of research.

The Graduate in Nashville is pink and lovely and country music themed. I really loved everything about it, from the high simmering Dolly Parton hero-worship to the cowboy boot print on the carpet. It is probably the most darling little hotel I’ve ever stayed at.

I really did imagine what it would be like if I didn’t go back home. What if I moved to this brand new city and taught myself how to die?

I mean what do I have to go back to? A world where I’m constantly ignoring the signals coming from inside and around me. At what point do I start listening? And at what point is it too late? Sometimes I feel like I’ve lost control of my life.

Is it a surprise that I feel like my brain and I are constantly at odds? I’m more often than not doing things I really don’t want to do. I need to learn how to honor my feelings and listen to what I really want. It’s difficult, but I need to start.

I feel, as most people probably do, that I could be doing so much more with my life. I want to live a life that I don’t need an escape from. But that’s all I do, escape in little and in large ways.

There's just so much I want to do! I want to make music, I want to make art, I want to have fun— I want to do something worthwhile! I know most people feel like this but doesn’t it just make you so frustrated? Why do we keep doing this?

It feels like you’re only living in small pockets in-between periods of being mostly dead.

Anyways, (let’s ignore the existential crisis) I tried finding a place in Nashville that sold zines, but couldn’t. I did, however, end up doing something print related when I went to Hatch Show Print.

Hatch has been around since 1879, originally across the street from the famous Ryman Auditorium, the shop has made posters for everyone from legends like Johnny Cash to contemporary artists like Kacey Musgraves. I also peeped an old Space Ghost Coast to Coast print they must’ve made in the 90’s for Adult Swim.

I had done linocut before, but I didn't know too much about letterpress. What I found really cool about Hatch is that a lot of the woodblocks they use are originals that were created by the actual members of the Hatch family, preserved for more than a handful of decades at this point. I think it’d be interesting to do a zine using letterpress someday.

I think the alternate reality I was accessing on this trip was one where I spend my days listening to music, making music, and just generally having fun.

I know it’s going to take me some time to map out what I’m trying to do, where I’m trying to go. It feels like there’s so much I’m trying to give birth to. How does it all fit together? How can I build the life I want? How does anyone?

At least for a little but, since I’ve been back, the world feels like it's full of magic and music. Not sure how long till it wears off but for now, it feels pretty good.
